My plans for Oyo university students as the Publicity Secretary (NAUS) OSUNTOKI OLAMILEKAN (NOATSBA)

Our Plans for the Growth and Visibility of NAUS

As a candidate for the position of Publicity Secretary under the National Association of University Students (NAUS), I present my vision of inclusivity, collaboration, and strategic growth that will elevate NAUS to new heights. Together, we will create a stronger, more visible, and impactful association for all students in Oyo State.

1. Collaboration Between Campuses: Collaboration is the bedrock of progress. My administration will:

  • Build a robust network of Public Relations Officers (PROs) across all universities in Oyo State to strengthen inter-campus communication.
  • Foster joint programs and initiatives that promote shared goals and address collective challenges among our member institutions.
  • Launch a unified calendar for campus events to ensure maximum participation and synergy.

Why this matters: Beneficial? YES! Stronger collaboration fosters unity and shared resources. Economical? YES! Pooled efforts reduce costs. Sustainable? YES! Relationships built today will serve future administrations. Feasible? YES! Existing communication channels and PROs will drive this forward.

2. Driving Inclusivity Through Programs Inclusivity ensures that no student or institution is left behind. My administration will:

  • Create diverse programs that cater to the academic, social, and cultural needs of all students.
  • Organize workshops and events that reflect the interests of various student demographics.
  • Partner with relevant organizations to ensure wider participation.

Why this matters: Beneficial? YES! Inclusivity fosters belonging. Economical? YES! Programs can attract sponsorships. Sustainable? YES! Inclusive events attract wider support. Feasible? YES! NAUS's influence ensures participation.

3. Visibility and Branding for NAUS A visible association is a strong association. My administration will:

  • Establish more platforms to amplify NAUS's voice, including newsletters, social media campaigns, and partnerships with media houses.
  • Rebrand NAUS with a stronger online presence, showcasing our activities and achievements.
  • Engage stakeholders in promoting NAUS initiatives to the broader community.

Why this matters: Beneficial? YES! A visible NAUS attracts opportunities. Economical? YES! Media partnerships lower costs. Sustainable? YES! Online platforms provide long-term visibility. Feasible? YES! Platforms are readily available.

  • Engaging with government bodies, NGOs, and private organizations for support and partnerships.
  • Advocating for policies and initiatives that benefit students.
  • Securing sponsorships to fund impactful programs.

Why this matters: Beneficial? YES! Partnerships drive development. Economical? YES! External funding reduces pressure on members. Sustainable? YES! Partnerships endure beyond a single term. Feasible? YES! Strong networks can be built.

  • Establishing a permanent NAUS office in Oyo State to serve as a hub for operations and a repository of history.
  • Creating a mentorship program for upcoming leaders in NAUS.

Why this matters: Beneficial? YES! A permanent office strengthens operations. Economical? YES! Consolidated resources lower costs. Sustainable? YES! A physical presence ensures continuity. Feasible? YES! Stakeholders will support this initiative.
